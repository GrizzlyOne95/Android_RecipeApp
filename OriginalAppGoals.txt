I am trying to make an android mobile recipe app for saving and managing recipes, while also functioning as a nutrition and calorie tracker per serving of food. 

Here are the features I want it to have:

Ability to browse and save online and manually entered recipes
ability scan barcodes to add the food items I actually use and their nutritional facts so that I can easily calculate a recipes calories/nutrition by selecting from items that I own (a “my pantry” section of the app
ability to scan food barcodes to auto-import nutrition facts/item pictures
automatically suggest saved pantry items to pull nutrition facts from in a recipe
ability to add a recipe as an ingredient in another (for example, being able to use nutrition facts of 1 serving of pie crust recipe in a quiche recipe)
Have 4 main tabs: Recipes (my saved recipes), Grocery List (where I can automatically add recipe ingredients for easy shopping), and Pantry (where I can scan/save the items I actually use in my pantry), and a Food Log tab (where you can track your calories/what you ate in a day)
In the Food Log, you can have a “saved meal” pop up with a dropdown menu where you can adjust the quantity of ingredients without affecting the master meal file.
Actual good recipe files where I can adjust the order of ingredients, add directions, have an auto scale function for multiplying a recipe
Ability to add individual ingredients to a meal plan, not just recipes (for example, Chili night would have the recipes cards for Chili and Cornbread, but I could also add sour cream to the list as well, so I can see all the components of the meal visually. 
Ability to have “version” tabs in a master recipe file so I can copy the master recipe, make changes to it, and save variations that I like
Ability to update a master recipe file, and when I do the app will automatically update the calorie count and nutrition facts of the other recipes/meals that contain that item (but also prompt an “Are you sure?” to make sure you don’t change info for something you don’t want to)
App automatically does the math for scale, weight, and unit conversions for you.
Ability to sort by calorie/serving: highest to lowest and also lowest to highest
Ability to pin master folders “e.g. a full meal plan folder) with ability to export to shopping list. 
Tracking for carbs, fats, protein, AND fiber in a meal and for the day
Suggested meals from your own meals and recipes to help fill in the macros you’re shooting for
Adjustable macro and calorie goals
Ability to “favorite” or add notes on particular scaling numbers (for example: single serve, Max for ninja foodie pot, etc)



My goal with this project is to make a recipe app that streamlines all the major tasks that go along with feeding oneself and/or family: planning meals and prep, grocery shopping, and making sure to have balanced, moderate meals. I also want it to be customizable and easy-to-use, with many different ways to sort, categorize, and visually organize the components in the app in a way that makes the most sense to the user.
